# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Two audiences, both Indian, both training-curious.

**Crew** — adults aged 22-45 in metros (Vizag, Hyderabad, Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi, Pune) who already train solo or want to start. They lift, run, ride, swim, stretch, hike. They are bored of doing it alone, frustrated by closed WhatsApp groups, and unwilling to commit to a chain gym subscription. They want a real crew at their pace, in their neighbourhood, this week.

**Organizers** — run club captains, yoga instructors, gym owners, calisthenics crews, hiking groups. Currently running their community out of seven WhatsApp groups, a Google Form, and a manual Excel sheet of waivers. They want one tool that handles RSVPs, check-ins, photo albums, and member messaging without enterprise pricing.

Context for both: scrolling on a phone, evaluating whether AltCrew is real and trustworthy in under 30 seconds. Pre-launch — they are deciding whether to join a waitlist or follow on Instagram.

## Product Purpose

AltCrew is India's social fitness app. It connects movement-curious people with the crews, classes, and clubs already happening near them, and gives community organizers the tools to run the operation.

Strava logs solo activity. WhatsApp keeps groups closed. AltCrew makes the third thing — finding actual humans to train with — public, searchable, and pace-matched.

Success at launch: 1000 verified crew members + 50 active organizers in Vizag by May 31, 2026, then expanding city by city.

## Brand Personality

Direct. Real. Energetic.

Voice: confident without bragging, warm without being cute. Sentence fragments OK. Short verbs. Skip throat-clearing.

Anti-influencer. Anti-corporate-wellness. Anti-LinkedIn-cringe. The brand sounds like a captain who has been running 6am tempo runs for ten years and just wants more people to show up.

Energy is earned through specificity (real cities, real sports, real crews) not through caps-lock or exclamation marks.

## Anti-references

**The Indian app aesthetic is the loudest "no".** Specifically:

- CRED-style heavy gradients, neon glow, dense feature stacks
- Cult.fit-style festival-banner overload — five offers competing for attention above the fold
- Generic Bollywood-bright orange-and-gold palettes
- Gradient text. Drop shadows on every button. Glassmorphism cards.
- Stock photography of laughing models in pristine athleisure
- "We are revolutionising your fitness journey" startup-speak
- Endless icon-grid feature lists with no hierarchy

Also avoid: Strava clone (black + neon graph), Whoop clone (data-forward dark), generic yoga app (beige + sage + italic serif lifestyle).

## Design Principles

1. **Earned, not decorated.** Every visual element must carry weight. No accents, gradients, or motion that exist only because the brief said "make it premium". If removing it doesn't hurt, remove it.

2. **Specificity beats polish.** Real city names, real sport names, real numbers. "Vizag" and "Marine Drive" beat "your city". "Pace-matched" beats "personalised". Specificity is what proves AltCrew exists.

3. **Show the human, not the product.** Photos of actual training, candid not staged. UI screenshots come second; people sweating come first.

4. **Two doors, one mission.** Organizer and crew flows are equal partners, not feature/upsell. The split is the product, not a marketing tactic.

5. **Pre-launch honesty.** Say "launching May 31" not "coming soon". Say "free for organizers through August 2026" not "no credit card required". Trust comes from specifics.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target: **WCAG 2.2 AA**.

- Text contrast ratio 4.5:1 minimum (3:1 for ≥18pt text)
- Full keyboard navigation, visible focus rings on all interactive surfaces
- `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` honored — mesh blob animation, marquee carousel, curtain reveals all halt or simplify
- All photos require alt text describing the activity, not just "fitness photo"
- Touch targets ≥48×48 px on mobile
- Form fields labelled, error messages tied via `aria-describedby`
- Page works without JavaScript for the core proposition + waitlist email capture
- Bilingual readiness: copy avoids idioms that don't translate to Hindi/Telugu when localised later
